Hydro Missile

"Its time to brace yourselves to prove to be a mechanical engineer.”

Join hands together to exhibit your talent in our TORQUE’19 Fest.

Water rocketing is a mechanical event that involves the fabrication of a rocket whose fuel thrust for flight is obtained by the use of water that is expelled at a large air pressure. It is an example of Newton’s third law of motion. The pre requisites for such an event would be a basic knowledge of fabrications, materials and physical calculations involving thrust, air pressure etc. The event tests a couple of aspects of the rocket’s flight. These include range, time of flight and accuracy of the launch machine.

Round 1

    Range Test:

  • Here the maximum range is tested. The distance between the launch pad and the point of landing is measured and taken to be the points in that round. Target consists of concentric circles with different radii. Based on radii points are allotted (given). Deviation is also calculated. With increase of deviation your points are reduced.

Round 2

    Time of flight test

  • Time of flight is taken into consideration. Points given are based on time of flight of each rocket.Only two chances are given for second round best of them is considered.

Rules

  • Rockets and its components must be handmade, no readymade rockets are allowed.
  • The rocket body must be made only with plastic bottles of aerated soft drinks of maximum capacity 2.5Lt and should be free by of any fabrication failures or dents caused by heat exposure. More than one bottle can be used but the total volume must be less than 2.5litre.
  • Each team must have their own launch pads.
  • No equipment for repair is provided so every team must bring their own equipment.
  • Two trials will be given to each team and best of two will be taken into consideration.
  • Use of electronic components and chemical substances are banned.
  • Extra points are awarded for an innovative thing in your design
  • The point where the rocket hits the ground will be taken for measurement.
    • NOTE: Range as well as Time of Flight is given equal importance.

    A maximum of four members are allowed per team.
